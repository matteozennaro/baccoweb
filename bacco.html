The BACCO project

The idea

A new generation of galaxy surveys will soon start measuring the spatial distribution of millions of galaxies over a broad range of redshifts, offering an imminent opportunity to discover new physics. A detailed comparison of these measurements with theoretical models of galaxy clustering may reveal a new fundamental particle, a breakdown of General Relativity, or a hint on the nature of cosmic acceleration.

Despite a significant progress in the analytic treatment of structure formation in recent years, traditional clustering models still suffer from large uncertainties. This limits cosmological analyses to a very restricted range of scales and statistics, which will be one of the main obstacles to reach a comprehensive exploitation of future surveys. With BACCO we propose to develop a novel simulation-based approach to predict galaxy clustering. Combining recent advances in computational cosmology, from cosmological N-body calculations to physically-motivated galaxy formation models, we will develop a unified framework to directly predict the positions and velocity of individual dark matter structures and galaxies as function of cosmological and astrophysical parameters.

In this formulation, galaxy clustering will be a prediction of a set of physical assumptions in a given cosmological setting. The new theoretical framework will be flexible, accurate and fast: it will provide predictions for any clustering statistic, down to scales 100 times smaller than in state-of-the-art perturbation--theory--based models, and in less than 1 minute of CPU time. These advances will enable major improvements in future cosmological constraints, which will significantly increase the overall power of future surveys maximising our potential to discover new physics.

Many ingredients...

Cosmology

A comprehensive and powerful cosmological calculator: set the parameters of your cosmological model, including massive neutrinos and dynamical dark energy, and start getting theoretical predictions for many possible observables. BACCO's cosmology class features a wrapper to call camb and class, implementations of methods to compute nonlinear quantities with perturbation theory or halofit for correlations functions, power spectra and bispectra, many models of mass functions, bias-mass and halo concentration-mass relations. Moreover, it integrates redshift space models accounting both for bulk motions and small-scale velocity dispersions.

Simulations

A lean version of Gadget, LG3, which allows us to create large simulations while keeping computing tame and RAM usage under control. Moreover, by computing many halo properties on the fly and by storing data in HDF5 format, we make the loading and analysis on simulations a simple and efficient task. See also the data page!

Mocks

How faithfully can we reproduce the properties of galaxies? Thanks to all the properties computed on-the-fly by our versiong of LG3, we have access to a wealth of information on the structures and substractures of our simulation. This enables us to produce state-of-the-art galaxy samples through subhalo abundance matching techinques, with much freedom to vary which physical quantities to match. Moreover, among the outputs of our simulations, we also store the entire merging history, making it easy to run semi-analytical models on the outputs of our simulations.

Neutrinos

Nowadays, a thorough implementation of the effects of massive neutrinos is fundamental in order to achieve the precision required by forthcoming cosmological observations. We payed extra-care in implementing neutrino effects in bacco, and we assumed the quasi-nonlinear Fourier space implementation of neutrinos in LG3.

Baryons

We cannot hope to push our predictions to small scales if we do not model the effects of baryons on the matter distribution! Bacco features a custom-made revised and efficient implementation of the baryon-correction model, in which dark matter particles of each halo are rearranged, according to some physically motivated prescriptions, to reproduce the density profile of the galaxy, hot gas and expelled gas. Thanks to this method, the dark matter distribution can be let relax and adapt to the presence of the baryon components, obtaining results in very good agreement with hydro-dynamical simulations.

Bayesian analysis

A new method to accurately reconstruct the shape of the multi-dimensional posterior distribution is one of the fundamental parts of bacco. This answers the need of modern cosmology for reconstructing the posterior distribution of the free parameters of the model while being very efficient in terms of number of likelihood evaluations needed. Therefore BACCO includes a likelihood emulator constructed with an iterative process, with the possibility of accounting for noise in the adopted theoretical models.

Visualization

Sometimes abstracting things and considering their statistical properties is just not enough: we are still speaking of objects that do exist in the universe around us! For both scientific and dissemination purposes it's important to visualize the matter density fields and appreciate their spatial distribution and the complexity of the cosmic web!
